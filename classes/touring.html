1/10 Touring Cars

Overview

Scale: 1/10
Drive: 4WD
Motor Limit: 13.5T Brushless
Battery: 2S Li-Po / 6-Cell NiMH

About This Class

1/10 Touring Cars are the most competitive and popular class at SMCC. These are 4WD electric touring cars using modern chassis and 13.5T brushless motors with rubber tyres. Racing is fast and exciting on the tight indoor circuit with close door-to-door battles and a focus on driving precision and setup. It’s a great option for experienced racers looking for high-performance RC action.

Setup Tips

  • Motor: Use a 13.5T brushless motor. Sensored systems are recommended with boost/turbo for competitive pace.
  • ESC: Open rules allow advanced tuning — make sure timing and boost are configured for indoor tracks.
  • Gearing: Gear your motor around FDR 4.0–5.0 based on track grip and power delivery.
  • Tyres: Schumacher U6793 (Front) and U6794 (Rear) in yellow compound. No tyre additive allowed.
  • Ride Height: Around 5mm front and rear. Lower the car for smooth tracks and higher grip.
  • Differentials: Front spool or very tight diff; rear gear diff with 5k–7k oil for stability and cornering.
  • Shocks: 40wt front, 35wt rear is a good starting point. Adjust for track bumps and responsiveness.
  • Chassis Balance: Add weights to achieve even left/right and front/rear distribution. Use a tweak station.
  • Body Shell: Mount low and level. Lightweight race bodies help reduce traction roll and increase corner speed.
